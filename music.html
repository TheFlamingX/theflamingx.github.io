Weezer Tierlist

A long, long time ago I made a tierlist on a wiki. I never finished it, but was suggested to finish it on my website. This is that tierlist. For the sake of simplicity, songs that are released exclusively through Alone or were only performed live (with no official recording), are not included.

Ranking

Separated into tiers. Ordered from best to worst.

Apotheosis

Think of this as the 99th percentile. Songs that are so good I avoid listening to them because if I listened to them every time I thought of them, I'd ruin it for myself.

  • Devotion
  • Tragic Girl
    • You're a tragic girl / You lead a tragic life / I'm just meant to be / Your latest tragedy
  • Susanne
    • When I met you I was all alone / Cold and hungry cryin' on the phone
    • The songs in apotheosis are hard to describe, since everything about them just works.

Peak

Think of this as above the 95th percentile. This is what comes to my mind when I want good Weezer music.

  • Unbreak My Heart
    • This is the only song EVER that I have liked the first time I listened to it. Every other song I have needed to listen to a couple of times.
  • I Just Threw Out the Love of My Dreams
    • OH HE'S GOT ME WONDERING / MY RIGHTEOUSNESS IS CRUMBLING / AND I SEE HIM EVERYDAY / EVEN THOUGH MY LOVE'S A WORLD AWAY
    • I'm a big fan of Pinkerton.
  • Waiting on You
  • Pink Triangle
  • I Woke Up in Love This Morning
    • Weezer has a knack for creating covers that are completely amazing and arguably eclipse the original. This is one of them. Unfortunately, it's a Japan-exclusive bonus track.
  • Mykel and Carli
    • I used to not really like this song... but then I developed ears

Good

Think of this as above the 80th percentile. I'll often go out of my way to listen to these songs when they cross my mind.

  • Everybody Needs Salvation
    • Fan Club was eating good with this one.
  • Falling for You
  • Miss Sweeney
  • Say It Ain't So
  • Lost in the Woods
  • Paperface
    • I actually prefer the Alone version of the song and its various additions compared to the version on Blue Deluxe.
  • Endless Bummer
  • Anonymous
  • QB Blitz
    • Real hot take: the only worthwhile song on Pacific Daydream
  • Represent
    • This is one of the rare times where I like what different mixes of a song bring to the table. I like the synth of the original and the subtle background tracks of the Rocked Out Mix.
  • Holiday
  • Only in Dreams
  • It's Been So Long
    • The man behind the slaughter
  • Buddy Holly

Solid

Think of this as above the 65th percentile. These are songs I occasionally think of, and go out of my way to listen to.

  • Jacked Up
  • Like a Good Neighbor
  • Trampoline
  • (Girl We Got a) Good Thing
  • In The Garage
  • Sugar Booger
    • Green has a massive amount of B-sides that never landed anywhere else. The songs would've probably been included on a Green Deluxe, but Green never got one, despite being the second(?) most successful album financially. But Hurley got a deluxe despite apparently being made in two weeks.
    • Of all of Green's B-sides, I think Sugar Booger is the best of them.
  • Undone - The Sweater Song
  • The World Has Turned and Left Me Here
    • I went to rank this song, and found that I had already ranked it in the exact spot I planned to rank it.

Alright

Think of this as above the 40th percentile. These are songs I don't really want to listen to, but I wholly don't mind listening to be getting the full experience of an album.

  • Jamie
  • Surf Wax America
  • Run Over By A Truck
    • I like the instrumental, but the lyrics are just a bit too nonsensical for me ("[Grandma] could teach a common hustler to dance, in France" Really?).
  • Do You Wanna Get High?
  • My Name is Jonas
    • Not actually a massive fan of Blue. Probably because of how played it is.
  • No One Else
    • Same logic as My Name is Jonas, but less catchy.

Awful

Think of this as below the 40th percentile.

  • Should I Stay or Should I Go
    • I don't really like live versions of songs. Should She Stay or Should She Go is better.
  • My Evaline
    • Bad B-side
  • Butterfly
  • I'm a Robot
    • I started listening to Weezer's songs by listening to two albums per week, from latest to earliest. Death to False Metal and Hurley shared a week, and this song single-handedly made me hate both albums for months.
  • Piece of Cake
    • One of the few Weezer songs I straight up do not recognize by name.
